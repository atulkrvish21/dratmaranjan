Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about neurosurgery and spine care
When should I see a neurosurgeon?

You should consult a neurosurgeon if you experience:

  • Severe or persistent headaches
  • Chronic back or neck pain
  • Numbness or tingling in arms/legs
  • Sudden weakness or loss of balance
  • Seizures or memory problems
  • Head or spine injury

Early consultation can prevent serious complications.

How long is recovery after brain surgery?

Recovery time depends on the type and complexity of surgery:

  • Hospital stay: 3-7 days
  • Initial recovery: 2-4 weeks
  • Full recovery: 6-12 weeks
  • Return to work: 4-8 weeks (varies by job type)

Dr. Dash provides personalized recovery plans and follow-up care.

What is minimally invasive spine surgery?

Minimally invasive spine surgery (MISS) uses smaller incisions (1-2 cm) compared to traditional open surgery. Benefits include:

  • Less muscle damage
  • Reduced blood loss
  • Faster recovery time
  • Shorter hospital stay (often 1-2 days)
  • Less post-operative pain

Dr. Dash is a pioneer in MISS techniques in Odisha.

Is spine surgery always necessary for back pain?

No, surgery is only recommended when:

  • Conservative treatments (medication, physiotherapy) fail after 6-8 weeks
  • There is progressive nerve damage or weakness
  • Severe pain affecting daily life
  • Loss of bladder or bowel control (emergency)

Dr. Dash always explores non-surgical options first.

What are the signs of a stroke?

Remember FAST:

  • F – Face drooping: One side of face numb or uneven smile
  • A – Arm weakness: One arm weak or drifting downward
  • S – Speech difficulty: Slurred speech or unable to speak
  • T – Time to call: Immediate medical attention needed

If you notice these signs, contact emergency services immediately. Early treatment saves lives!
